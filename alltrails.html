Overview

When community data becomes a safety problem

AllTrails' strength is its crowd-sourced database of over 400,000 trails contributed by an active community of 65 million users. But that same model is its most significant liability: unverified, user-generated data regularly leads hikers off trail, provides inaccurate time and distance estimates, and in some cases, into genuine danger.

This project, completed as part of the CMU Master of Integrated Innovation for Products and Services program, set out to identify how AllTrails could use its own community to fix the problem, by designing a feature set that turns every user into a contributor to data integrity.

The process moved from user research through ecology mapping, jobs-to-be-done analysis, and high-fidelity prototype design, culminating in a tested feature concept validated with real users.

The Feature Design

Three interconnected features form the core concept: a post-hike accuracy rating system, a crowd-sourced verification loop, and a contributor profile with gamified incentives to drive participation.
