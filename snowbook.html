The Problem

Avalanche Forecasters Are Making Critical Decisions on Incomplete Data

Data collection in mountain environments is genuinely hard. Access is remote, power sources are often nonexistent, and the terrain that matters most is exactly the terrain that is most difficult to instrument. In winter, this data scarcity means avalanche forecasters and snow scientists are left to rely on inconsistent field observations, weather model outputs, and a sparse network of fixed weather stations to make snowpack assumptions across enormous, variable terrain.

The consequences of that gap are significant. Forecast accuracy depends directly on the spatial density of snowpack data. Where data is thin, uncertainty is high. And where uncertainty is high, the margin for error in hazard communication narrows.

More data points, better spatial awareness, and consistent delivery from locations no fixed station will ever reach.

Snowbook was designed to close that gap directly: a network of low-cost, deployable snow sensors that can be placed anywhere, run on battery power for a month at a time, and transmit measurements automatically every 30 minutes without requiring field personnel to be present.

Use Cases

Three Audiences, One Platform

Snowbook is designed around three distinct professional use cases, each of which benefits from the same core capability: reliable, high-resolution snowpack data from locations that were previously unmeasured.

1
Professional Avalanche Forecasters

Deploy sensors across forecast zones to increase spatial resolution of snowpack data. Improved data density feeds directly into hazard rating accuracy and the quality of public safety communication.

2
Research Scientists

Instrument remote terrain for climate, meteorology, and hydrology research. Snowbook enables consistent, longitudinal data collection in locations previously accessible only through expensive and time-intensive manual measurements.

3
Ski Resorts

Gain higher-fidelity understanding of snowpack conditions across ski area terrain and assess the effectiveness of avalanche mitigation efforts with real measurement data rather than estimates.

How It Works

Deploy, Measure, Transmit, Predict

Snowbook units are installed by field technicians across locations where snowpack data is needed. From that point, the system operates autonomously. Snow depth and temperature measurements are collected continuously and transmitted to a central database via SMS every 30 minutes, with no requirement for local power infrastructure or network connectivity beyond a cellular signal.

Battery life supports approximately 30 days of continuous operation before a technician returns to swap the battery or collect the unit. That runtime is long enough to capture the arc of a storm cycle and its aftermath without mid-deployment intervention.

On the software side, incoming sensor data flows into two output streams. Raw measurements are made available directly for specialist use. In parallel, the data feeds Snowbook's machine learning model, which combines sensor inputs with standard geo-relevant datasets to generate high-integrity predictions of avalanche hazard and snowpack structure.

Hardware
  • Ultrasonic distance sensor for snow depth
  • Mobile snow thermometer array
  • On-board computer with cellular transmitter
  • Robust, weatherproof field enclosure
  • Battery-powered, approximately 30-day runtime
Software & AI
  • SMS data transmission every 30 minutes
  • XGBoost machine learning model
  • 5-scale avalanche hazard rating by location
  • Weak layer and reactive snowpack detection
  • Raw data API for specialist access
Key Distinctions

What Sets Snowbook Apart from Fixed Weather Stations

Spatial Resolution

More data points across variable terrain produces fundamentally better spatial awareness of snowpack conditions than a fixed network ever can.

Cost

Snowbook units cost pennies on the dollar compared to standardized fixed snow plot stations, making broad deployment financially viable for the first time.

Consistency

Automated 30-minute transmission cycles deliver data continuously, without the gaps that come with resource-intensive and weather-dependent manual measurements.

AI-Powered Prediction

The XGBoost model connects field measurements to hazard ratings and snowpack structure predictions, bridging raw sensor data and actionable safety intelligence.
